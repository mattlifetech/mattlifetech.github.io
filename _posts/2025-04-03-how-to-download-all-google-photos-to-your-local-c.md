### How to Download All Google Photos to Your Local Computer

![image](/assets/images/medium/how-to-download-all-google-photos-to-your-local-c-0.png)


Google Photos is a great cloud storage service, but having a local backup of your photos and videos is always a good idea. Whether you’re moving to another service or just want an offline copy, here’s a step-by-step guide on how to download all your Google Photos to your local computer.
### Method 1: Using Google Takeout

Google Takeout is the official tool for exporting all your Google Photos.
#### Step 1: Access Google Takeout

1. Go to [Google Takeout](https://takeout.google.com/).
1. Sign in with your Google account if you haven’t already.
#### Step 2: Select Google Photos

1. Click **“Deselect all”** to avoid downloading unnecessary data.
1. Scroll down and check **Google Photos**.
1. Click **“All photo albums included”** to choose specific albums or leave it selected to download everything.
#### Step 3: Choose File Format and Delivery Method

1. Select **.zip** or **.tgz** as the file format.
1. Choose the archive size (e.g., 2GB, 10GB, 50GB). If your photos exceed this limit, multiple files will be created.
1. Choose a delivery method:

- Email with a download link
- Add to Google Drive, Dropbox, OneDrive, or Box
#### Step 4: Request the Download

1. Click **“Create Export”**.
1. Google will process your request (this may take minutes to days depending on the size of your library).
1. Once ready, you’ll receive an email with a link to download your files.
#### Step 5: Download and Extract Files

1. Open the email from Google and click **“Download your files”**.
1. Extract the `.zip` or `.tgz` files to access your photos and videos.

---

### Method 2: Downloading Manually from Google Photos

If you only need to download specific photos or albums, you can do it manually.
#### Step 1: Open Google Photos

1. Go to [Google Photos](https://photos.google.com/).
1. Sign in to your Google account.
#### Step 2: Select and Download Photos

1. Click on individual photos or use **Shift + Click** to select multiple images.
1. Click the **three-dot menu** (⋮) in the top-right corner.
1. Select **“Download”** — This will download the photos as a `.zip` file.
#### Step 3: Download Entire Albums

1. Open an album.
1. Click the **three-dot menu** (⋮) and select **“Download all”**.
1. The album will be compressed into a `.zip` file for download.

---

### Method 3: Using Google Takeout with a Direct Sync

If you frequently back up your photos, you can use Google Takeout with a cloud service:

1. Follow Steps 1–3 in **Method 1**.
1. Select **“Add to Google Drive”** (or another cloud service).
1. Set up a local sync using **Google Drive for Desktop**.
1. Your photos will automatically sync to a folder on your computer.

### Final Tips

- **Downloaded image retained Photo Taken info**: You can use any photo to manage and view it on the same date or in the same order.
- **Use an external drive**: If your collection is large, store backups on an external hard drive.
- **Verify downloaded files**: Open some images to ensure they downloaded correctly.

By following these steps, you can successfully download and safeguard your Google Photos on your local computer. Happy backing up!
