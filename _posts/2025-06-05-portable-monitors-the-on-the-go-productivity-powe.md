### Portable Monitors: The On-the-Go Productivity Powerhouse
#### But What About Their Lifespan?

![image](/assets/images/medium/portable-monitors-the-on-the-go-productivity-powe-0.png)

*Created by Author via Gemini*

### The Nice Things About Portable Monitors

They work as intended. They are lightweight, easy to carry around, and extremely easy to set up next to your laptop for productive work.

I have purchased three units of portable monitors. They last about two years before they break. The reason for this will be covered below.

---

### Portable Monitors Are Not All the Same

Do not simply choose any model. First, identify the main device you plan to use with the portable monitor.
#### Main device:

- **MacBook **— can support USB-C video input & a no-battery version
- **PC** — unless you are using a GPU with USB-C like RTX 20 series, you should choose mini-HDMI video input & a no-battery version
- **Windows Laptop with USB-C DP Alt Mode** — choose the version without a built-in battery and USB-C video input.
- **Windows Laptop without USB-C DP Alt Mode** —choose the version with a built-in battery and mini-HDMI video input
- **PS5 or Xbox Series** — choose mini-HDMI video input & have a battery version
- **Smartphones with USB-C DP Alt Mode —** choose mini-HDMI video input & have a battery version.

I generally recommend the no-battery version to avoid charging management hassle or battery degradation issues in the long run.

The battery-version portable monitor is recommended only if you are using a **Windows laptop without USB-C DP Alt Mode**, to **reduce cable clutter**.
#### Other features you may care about:

- **Size 10 to 18 inches** — depends on your travel carrier for your main device. Example 13-inch laptop bag = choose 13 13-inch portable monitor
- **Resolution 1080P or 4K **— I recommend choosing a 1080P resolution, as both Mac and PC operating systems provide a more user-friendly display of icons on the desktop and within applications compared to 4K monitors, especially for portable monitors under 18 inches in size.
- **Refresh Rate** — 60FPS is sufficient. You are unlikely to perform high-end gaming on this portable monitor
- **HDR** — match your main device settings. (Screenshots in HDR mode will make the image washed out)
- **IPS or OLED panel**
- **Touchscreen or stylus support **— optional = it is not comfortable to use.

---

### Is there an alternative?

Yes, there are alternatives, but generally for an **environmental setting with WiFi**.

> Again, devices and brands are very siloed nowadays. Do validate before purchasing a new device. Some devices are natively supported, while others require a 3rd party app.
#### These devices can be used as a second screen if supported:

- iOS Tablet
- Android Tablet
- Android Foldable Smartphones.

---

### Why does the Portable Monitor’s lifespan only last about two years?
#### 1. Quality

I didn't buy the most expensive models. Each cost me around $100-$150 per monitor.

The build and touch quality are good. Image quality is good as well.
#### 2. Travel

Even if the portable monitor is built with a metal casing, it is very slim. When traveling, a portable monitor is at **risk of bending** if not kept properly. This, in the long run, can break the monitor.
#### 3. Used as a fixed stationary monitor

When I’m not traveling, I use it as my “fixed” secondary monitor. I’m not sure if this is a valid concern, but two of my previous monitors failed when used in this way.

***Suspected reasons ***— Poor **heat dissipation** & USB-C output to the monitor might be **powered on all the time.**
#### 4. Dishonest Seller

Some sellers honestly inform you that they are using refurbished screens from old laptops to build their portable monitors. If you bought a “new” monitor but it suffered a short lifespan, this may be the reason.

---

### What was my last take?

Portable monitors are nice to use, but:

1. I am *not going to buy* a portable monitor anymore
1. I stick with ***MacBook and Xiaomi tablet ***for a second screen (when out of home, turn on phone ***Hotspot ***to create a WiFi environment for casting).
