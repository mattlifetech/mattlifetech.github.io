### Ditch the Drawer of Dongles
#### Why One Charger Still Can’t Rule Them All (Yet) [Guide Included]

![image](/assets/images/medium/ditch-the-drawer-of-dongles-799e4a14dfd8-0.png)

*Created by Author via Gemini*


**The dream of a single, super-charger to power every gadget in your home is tantalizing. But while universal standards are making progress, the quest for ultimate charging speed still has some proprietary strings attached.**

In our modern lives, we’re swimming in a sea of devices. Smartphones, tablets, laptops, smartwatches, wireless earbuds — the list goes on. And each one typically comes with its own dedicated charger, leading to a tangled mess of cables and a drawer overflowing with power bricks. The idea of a single, universal charger capable of efficiently powering everything is incredibly appealing. Imagine the decluttering! The travel simplicity!

The good news is that universal charging standards like **USB Power Delivery (USB-PD)** are making significant strides. With USB-PD, a single charger can intelligently negotiate the power needs of various devices, delivering the right amount of juice without frying your delicate smartwatch or starving your power-hungry laptop. This means you *can* often use a single, high-wattage USB-PD charger to power many of your gadgets.

However, the dream of truly universal *fastest* charging still faces a significant hurdle: **proprietary charging protocols**.

Many smartphone manufacturers, particularly in the Android ecosystem, have developed their own fast-charging technologies that go above and beyond the standard USB-PD. Think of **Xiaomi’s HyperCharge**, **Oppo’s VOOC/Warp Charge/Dart Charge**, and **Huawei’s SuperCharge**. These protocols often boast impressive charging speeds, filling up your battery in a fraction of the time compared to standard charging or even basic USB-PD.

**The Catch: Why Third-Party “Super Chargers” Fall Short**

While you might find third-party chargers that advertise high wattage and compatibility with various brands, you’ll often discover that you don’t achieve the *absolute fastest* charging speeds advertised by your phone manufacturer. Here’s why:

1. **Proprietary Communication is Key:** These super-fast charging technologies rely on a specific communication handshake between the official charger and the phone. This communication, often facilitated by specialized chips in both the charger and the cable, allows them to operate at higher voltages or, more commonly, significantly higher currents (amperage) than the USB-PD standard typically allows. Third-party chargers, even those with high wattage outputs, generally don’t implement this exact proprietary communication protocol.
1. **Specialized Power Delivery:** Protocols like VOOC and SuperCharge often shift much of the power conversion process from the phone to the charger. This design helps to keep the phone cooler during rapid charging. Their proprietary chargers are specifically engineered to deliver power in this unique way, and a generic high-wattage charger won’t replicate this architecture.
1. **Cable Dependency:** The official cables that come with these super-charging phones are often specifically designed to handle the high currents involved safely and efficiently. They might have thicker wires or additional internal components. Using a standard USB-C cable with a third-party charger might limit the charging speed or even pose a safety risk if it’s not rated for the required current.
1. **Safety and Optimization:** Phone manufacturers meticulously design and test their proprietary charging systems for optimal safety and battery health. Deviating from this ecosystem with a third-party charger, even one with similar wattage, might not offer the same level of safety features or battery optimization.

**In Conclusion**

While the convenience of a universal USB-PD charger for many of your devices is a welcome reality, achieving the absolute fastest charging speeds often requires sticking with the official charger and cable that came with your phone, especially for brands employing proprietary “super charging” technologies.

The dream of a single charger to rule them all isn’t fully realized yet, particularly if you prioritize shaving every possible minute off your charging time. However, the increasing adoption of USB-PD is a significant step in the right direction, offering a more streamlined and interoperable charging experience for the majority of our daily tech. For now, understanding the charging protocols your devices support is the key to making informed decisions and minimizing that dreaded drawer of dongles.
