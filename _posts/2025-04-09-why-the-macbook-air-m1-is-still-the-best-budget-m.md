### Why the MacBook Air M1 Is Still the Best Budget MacBook You Can Buy in 2025

![image](/assets/images/medium/why-the-macbook-air-m1-is-still-the-best-budget-m-0.png)


The MacBook Air M1 was Apple’s first step into the world of custom silicon — and what a step it was. Launched in 2020, it set new standards for battery life, performance, and efficiency in a fanless laptop.

Now, five years later in 2025, Apple has moved on to the M3 and M4 chip lines. But here’s the twist: the M1 MacBook Air still makes a **compelling case as the best budget-friendly laptop**, especially if you’re eyeing a reliable second-hand Mac.

---

### 🚀 Performance: Not the Newest, But Still Smooth

Sure, the M3 chip brings faster speeds and stronger GPU performance, especially in graphics-heavy workflows. But for **everyday tasks** — think browsing, Netflix, Office apps, light coding, Zoom meetings — the M1 still flies.

Benchmarks say it’s about **60% slower than M3**, but in daily life? It’s fast enough that most users won’t feel the difference unless you’re a power user.

---

### 🖥️ Work-Ready: Docs, Calls, and Calm Multitasking

For students, writers, remote workers, and office warriors, the M1 MacBook Air still hits all the right notes. It handles documentation, spreadsheets, multitasking, Slack, and video calls with ease.

The webcam and mic setup may not be groundbreaking in 2025, but they’re still **plenty good** for Zoom or Teams calls. And the **silent, fanless design** means no annoying whirring while you’re on a call or deep in a deadline.

---

### 🔋 Battery Life: Still Holding Up After All These Years

One of the MacBook Air M1’s most underrated strengths is how well its battery holds up over time.

Many users in 2025 report **battery health around 85–90%** after years of daily use. And even with some degradation, it still delivers around **6–10 hours of use per charge** — more than enough for work, class, or travel days.

And if you snag a second-hand unit with a worn battery? Apple still offers battery replacements to refresh your machine without breaking the bank.

---

### 🤖 Yes, Even AI Can Run on the M1

With the rise of local AI tools, the M1’s relevance has actually *increased*. Lightweight LLMs like **TinyLLaMA, Whisper, and ChatGPT-compatible engines** can run just fine on the M1 chip.

It’s not going to replace a dedicated workstation, but for learning, testing, and hobby projects, the M1 holds its own in 2025.

---

### 💡 Verdict: A Smart Buy for Smart People

If the M3 or M4 MacBook Airs feel out of budget, **don’t overlook the M1**. Second-hand units now go for **$500–700 USD**, and for that price, you’re getting a machine that:

- Is fast enough for most tasks
- Runs quietly and efficiently
- Still has great battery life
- Supports macOS Sonoma (and probably a couple more updates to come)

If you’re a student, creator on a budget, remote worker, or someone who just wants a reliable, minimalist laptop — the **MacBook Air M1 is still a fantastic buy** in 2025.
